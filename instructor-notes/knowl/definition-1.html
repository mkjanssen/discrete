Definition 4.1.1.

Let \(X,Y\) be nonempty sets. A function (or map, or mapping) is a relation \(f\) on \(X\times Y\) in which each element \(x\in X\) is related to exactly one element \(y\in Y\text{.}\) We write \(y = f(x)\) and \(f : X\to Y\text{.}\) We say that \(X\) is the domain of \(f\text{,}\) \(Y\) is the codomain of \(f\text{,}\) \(y\) is the image of \(x\) under \(f\text{,}\) and the set \(\ran(f) = \setof{y\in Y}{\exists x\in X, y=f(x)}\) is the range of \(f\text{.}\)
