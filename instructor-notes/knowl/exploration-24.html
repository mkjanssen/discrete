Exploration 5.2.2.

Consider the family of sets \(X_n = \set{1,2,\ldots,n}\) for \(n\ge 1\text{.}\)

  1. Explicitly calculate \(\card{\pow(X_n)}\) for \(1 \le n \le 3\text{.}\)
  2. What do you think \(\card{\pow(X_4)}\) will be? Either make an argument or explicitly verify your guess.
  3. If you write a number in binary, you only use 0's and 1's (which in this context are called binary digits, or bits. We'll call a string of 0's and 1's a bit-string. Examples of bit-strings are things like 0101, 011100111, etc. How many bit-strings of length 4 are there?
  4. What do the previous two questions have to do with one another?
  5. Make a conjecture for \(\card{\pow(X_n)}\) in terms of \(n\text{.}\)
