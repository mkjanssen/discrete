Item 2.
How many ways are there to make an \(n\)-person committee chosen \(n\) Dordt students and \(n\) Northwestern students? On the one hand, \(\binom{2n}{n}\text{.}\) On the other hand, recall that \(\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}\text{,}\) so the sum can be rewritten as
\begin{equation*} \sum\limits_{k=0}^n \left(\binom{n}{k}\right)^2 = \sum\limits_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} \binom{n}{n-k}. \end{equation*}
Let \(k\) be the number of Dordt students. Then for each \(k\) we choose \(k\) Dordt students in \(\binom{n}{k}\) ways and \(n-k\) NWC students in \(\binom{n}{n-k}\) ways.
