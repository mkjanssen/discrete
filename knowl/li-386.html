Item 2.
Suppose Al gets 4 pies. Now he is guaranteed too many. There are 7 pies left to distribute among the four children; there are \(\binom{7 + 3}{3} = 120\) ways for Al to get too many pies. Since there are 4 children, there are \(\binom{4}{1} \cdot \binom{10}{3}\) ways for at least one child to get too many pies.
