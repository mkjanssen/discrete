If \(x=b\text{,}\) then all vertices in \(G'\) have even degree. Therefore, by induction, \(G'\) has an Euler circuit \(W\) that begins and ends at \(b\text{.}\) If we insert the edge \(ab\) at the beginning of \(W\text{,}\) we have constructed an Euler path that begins at \(a\) and ends at \(b\text{.}\)
