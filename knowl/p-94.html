The last way we'll combine sets \(A\) and \(B\) involves making ordered pairs. We say the Cartesian product of \(A\) with \(B\) is the set of all ordered pairs \((a,b)\) in which \(a\in A\) and \(b\in B\text{.}\) That is, \(A \times B = \{(a,b) \st a \in A \wedge b \in B\}\text{.}\)
