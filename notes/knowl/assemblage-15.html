Union and Intersection.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets. The union of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A\cup B\text{,}\) is the set containing all elements \(x\) which are in \(A\) or \(B\) (or both). The intersection \(A\cap B\) is the set of all elements of \(x\) in both \(A\)and\(B\text{.}\)
