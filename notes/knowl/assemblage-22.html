Partitions.

Let \(X\) be a set. Then a partition \(\mathfrak{P}\) of \(X\) is a subsets of \(\pow(X)\) satisfying:

  1. Each element of \(\mathfrak{P}\) is nonempty
  2. If \(x\in X\text{,}\) then there is some \(S\in \mathfrak{P}\) with \(x\in S\)
  3. The family \(\mathfrak{P}\) is pairwise disjoint: if \(S,T\in \mathfrak{P}\text{,}\) then \(S\cap T = \emptyset\)
