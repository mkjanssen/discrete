Definining a function requires three elements: a domain, a codomain, and an assignment of each member of the domain to a unique member of the codomain. As your experience suggests, there are several ways to represent that assignment, such as via a “rule” (e.g., those defined by algebraic expressions, though not all algebraic expressions define functions); via a graphical relationship; via a table, or in words (e.g., the birthday function).
