Let \(m\) be an even integer. By DefinitionÂ 3.1.1, there exists an integer \(k\) such that \(m = 2k\text{.}\) When we square \(m\text{,}\) we obtain \(m^2 = (2k)^2 = 2^2 \cdot k^2 = 4k^2\text{.}\) Observe that we may rewrite \(m^2\) as

\begin{equation*} m^2 = 4k^2 = 2(2k^2). \end{equation*}

Since \(2k^2\) is an integer, we have written \(m^2\) as the product of 2 and an integer. Therefore, \(m^2\) is even.
