Suppose \(m\) is even but, for a contradiction, that \(m^2\) is odd. Then there exist integers \(j,k\) such that \(m = 2j\) and \(m^2 = 2k+1\text{.}\) We observe that \(m^2 = m\cdot m = (2j) \cdot (2j) = 2(2j^2) = 2k+1\text{.}\) Thus, \(m^2\) is both even and odd, in clear violation of AxiomÂ 3.1.10. We therefore find that \(m^2\) cannot be odd, and must be even.
