Relations.

Let \(S,T\) be sets. A relation\(\mathcal{R}\) on \(S\) and \(T\) is a subset of \(S\times T\text{.}\) That is, \(\mathcal{R}\subseteq S\times T\text{.}\) We write \((s,t)\in \mathcal{R}\) to say that \(s\) is related to \(t\) under the relation. We sometimes write \(s \mathcal{R} t\text{.}\) The relation class of \(x\in S\) is the set of everything related to \(x\) from \(T\text{:}\) \([x] = \setof{y\in T}{x\mathcal{R}y}\text{.}\)
