Injective, Surjective, and Bijective Functions.

A function is injective provided every element of the codomain is the image of at most one element from the domain. An injective function is often called an injection.

A function is surjective provided every element of the codomain is the image of at least one element from the domain. A surjective function is often called a surjection.

A function that is both injective and surjective is called bijective, and referred to as a bijection.
