Definition 15.2.1.

Let \(G\) be a graph. A vertex \(v\in V(G)$\) is called a cut vertex of \(G\) provided \(G\setminus v\) (that is, \(G\) with \(v\) and any incident edges removed) has more components than \(G\text{.}\)

Similarly, an edge \(e\in E(G)\) is called a cut edge of \(G\) provided \(G\setminus e\) has more components than \(G\text{.}\)
