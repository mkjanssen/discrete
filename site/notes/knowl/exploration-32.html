Exploration 7.2.1.

Use a combinatorial proof to establish the following identities. Bonus points for humor and creativity.

  1. \begin{equation*} \binom{w}{p} \binom{p}{m} = \binom{w}{m} \binom{w-m}{p-m} \end{equation*}
  2. \begin{equation*} \sum\limits_{k=0}^n \left(\binom{n}{k}\right)^2 = \binom{2n}{n} \end{equation*}
  3. \begin{equation*} \binom{n+m}{r} = \sum\limits_{i=0}^r \binom{n}{i} \binom{m}{r-i} \end{equation*}
