Investigation 10.2.1.

Let \(P(n)\) represent the open statement

4 divides \(5^n - 1\)
  1. Does this open statement become a true statement when \(n=1\text{?}\) That is, is \(P(1)\) true?
  2. Does this open statement become a true statement when \(n=2\text{?}\) That is, is \(P(2)\) true?
  3. Choose at least four more natural numbers and determine whether the open statement is true or false for each of your choices.
  4. Suppose now that we know that \(P(k)\) is true. Can we use this fact to argue that \(P(k+1)\) is true?
