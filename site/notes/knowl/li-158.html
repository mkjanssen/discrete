Item 3.
Let \(X = \set{1,\ldots, 10}\text{,}\) \(X_n = \set{2n-1,2n}\) for \(n=1,2,\ldots, 5\text{.}\) Convince yourself that
\begin{equation*} X = X_1 \cup X_2 \cup \cdots \cup X_5. \end{equation*}
Then, define a relation \(\mathcal{R}\) on \(X\) by \((x,y)\in \mathcal{R}\) iff there exists \(i\in \set{1,2,\ldots,5}\) for which \(x,y\in X_i\text{.}\)
