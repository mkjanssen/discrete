The last two rows of Table 9 are also worth a moment of our time. They state that if the statement \(P\) 2  is false, then the implication \(P\Rightarrow Q\) is true. Note that this is different than saying that \(Q\) 3  is true. When the implication \(P\Rightarrow Q\) is true because \(P\) is false, we usually say that \(P\Rightarrow Q\) is vacuously true.
