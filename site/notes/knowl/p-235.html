The main problem is to figure out the number of choices for toppings. We will assume no repeated toppings (or at least that, e.g., double pepperoni is the same as single pepperoni for the purposes of counting combinations of toppings). We also assume that the order in which toppings are added does not matter (possibly contentious!). Note that for \(0 \le k \le 15\text{,}\) there are \(\binom{15}{k}\) ways to make a \(k\)-topping pizza. Then there are

\begin{equation*} 3\cdot 3\cdot \sum\limits_{k=0}^{15} \binom{15}{k} = 294,912 \end{equation*}

possible pizzas.
