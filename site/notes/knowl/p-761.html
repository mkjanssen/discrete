A finite graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a subgraph that is a subdivision

Subdivision is the process of replacing an edge \(e\) in a graph with a new vertex “in between” the vertices incident to the two ends of \(e\text{,}\) and new edges incident to the new vertex and the ends of \(e\text{.}\)
