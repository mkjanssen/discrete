Now consider the graph \(G' = G\setminus e\text{.}\) This graph has exactly two components. Since the path \(P\) does not use \(e\text{,}\) vertices \(a\) and \(b\) are in the same component. Notice also that, in \(G'\text{,}\) vertex \(a\) has even degree, and all other vertices in its component have not changed degree. This means that, in \(G'\text{,}\) there is exactly one vertex of odd degree, which is a contradiction.
