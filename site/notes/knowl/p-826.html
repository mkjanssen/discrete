If \(x\ne b\text{,}\) then \(G'\) has exactly two vertices of odd degree (\(x\) and \(b\)) and \(m\) edges. Thus by induction there is an Euler path \(W\) that begins at \(x\) and ends at \(b\text{.}\) If we prepend the edge \(ax\) to the beginning of \(W\) we have an Euler path that begins at \(a\) and ends at \(b\text{.}\)
