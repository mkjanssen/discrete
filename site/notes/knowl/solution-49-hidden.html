Proof: We will use a proof by contradiction. So we assume that the proposition is false, which means there exist real numbers \(x,y\) where \(x\notin \Q\text{,}\) \(y\in \Q\text{,}\) and \(x+y\in \Q\text{.}\) Since the rational numbers are closed under subtraction and \(x+y,y\in\Q\text{,}\) we see that

\begin{equation*} (x+y)-y\in\Q. \end{equation*}

However, \((x+y)-y = x\text{,}\) and hence we can conclude that \(x\in\Q\text{.}\) This is a contradiction to the assumption that \(x\notin \Q\text{.}\) Therefore, the proposition is false, and we have proven that for all real numbers \(x,y\text{,}\) if \(x\) is irrational and \(y\) is rational, then \(x+y\) is irrational.
