Theorem 15.2.4.

Let \(G\) be a connected graph. If every vertex of \(G\) has even degree, then for any \(v\in V(G)\text{,}\) there is an Euler circuit that begins and ends at \(v\text{.}\) Alternatively, if \(G\) has exactly two vertices of odd degree, say \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) there is an Euler path that begins at \(a\) and ends at \(b\text{.}\)
